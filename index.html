Cassava Cake Recipe

Cassava Cake is a classic Filipino dessert made from grated cassava (manioc). Cassava is also known as kamoteng kahoy and balinghoy in the Philippines. Cassava starch is also used to make tapioca.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs cassava grated
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 6 oz evaporated milk
  • 3 pieces egg
  • 1/4 cup butter melted
  • 6 tablespoons cheddar cheese grated
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 14 tablespoons granulated white sugar

ToppingIngredients:

  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoons cheddar cheese grated
  • 2 cups coconut milk

Instructions:

  • 1. Make the batter by combining the grated cassava, butter, 1/2 cup condensed milk, 6 oz. evaporated milk, 6 tablespoons cheddar cheese, 14 tablespoons sugar, and 2 eggs in a mixing bowl and mix thoroughly.2 lbs cassava, 6 oz evaporated milk, 3 pieces egg, 1/4 cup butter, 1/2 cup condensed milk, 14 tablespoons granulated white sugar, 1/2 cup condensed milk, 6 tablespoons cheddar cheese
  • 2. Add the 2 cups coconut milk in the mixing bowl where the mixed ingredients are. Mix again.2 cups coconut milk
  • 3. Grease the baking tray then pour-in the batter (these are the ingredients that you just mixed together).
  • 4. Pre -heat oven for 350 degrees Fahrenheit for 10 minutes then put-in the baking tray with batter and bake for 1 hour.Remove from the oven and set aside.
  • 5. Meanwhile prepare the topping by combining 2 tablespoons sugar and flour in the heated saucepan.2 tablespoons sugar, 2 tablespoons flour
  • 6. Pour-in 1/2 cup condensed milk then mix thoroughly.1/2 cup condensed milk
  • 7. Add 2 tablespoons cheddar cheese while stirring constantly.2 tablespoons cheddar cheese
  • 8. Pour 2 cups of coconut milk and stir constantly for 10 minutes2 cups coconut milk
  • 9. Pour the topping over the Cassava Cake (baked batter) and spread evenly.
  • 10. Separate the yolk from the egg white of the remaining egg (weâ€™ll be needing the egg white only)
  • 11. Glaze the topping with the egg white using a basting brush. Simply dip the brush to the egg white and brush it on the cassava cake.
  • 12. Set your oven to broil mode. Broil the Cassava cake until color turns light brown.
  • 13. Garnish with extra grated cheese on top.
